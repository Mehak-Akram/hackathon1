## Introduction

This textbook serves as a comprehensive guide to the rapidly evolving fields of Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics. It is designed for students, researchers, and engineers seeking to understand the theoretical foundations, practical applications, and future directions of intelligent robots that interact with the physical world. We will explore key concepts ranging from fundamental robotics principles and simulation environments to advanced topics like vision-language-action (VLA) models and real-world deployment of autonomous humanoids.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:
- Define Physical AI and its importance in modern robotics.
- Understand the core concepts of humanoid robotics and their applications.
- Identify the interdisciplinary nature of physical AI, encompassing mechanical engineering, computer science, and artificial intelligence.

## Key Concepts

- **Physical AI**: The branch of artificial intelligence focused on intelligent agents that perceive, reason, and act within the physical world, often embodied in robotic systems.
- **Humanoid Robotics**: The study and development of robots designed to resemble the human body, capable of performing human-like tasks and interacting with human-centric environments.
- **Interdisciplinary Nature**: The requirement for integrating knowledge from various fields such as mechanical design, control theory, computer vision, natural language processing, and machine learning to create effective physical AI systems.
